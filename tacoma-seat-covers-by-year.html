Not all seat covers fit all Tacomas. The 2nd generation (2005–2015) and 3rd generation (2016–2023) have different seat shapes, headrest styles, and mounting points. This guide breaks down what fits your specific truck.

3rd Generation Tacoma (2016–2023)

The 3rd gen brought a completely redesigned interior with new seat shapes, integrated headrests on some trims, and updated side airbag locations. You cannot use 2nd gen covers on a 3rd gen truck.

Key Differences Between Generations

  • Seat shape: 3rd gen seats are wider with more bolstering
  • Headrests: Some 3rd gen trims have integrated (non-removable) headrests
  • Airbags: Side airbag locations changed in 2016 — covers MUST be compatible
  • Rear bench: 3rd gen rear bench folds differently than 2nd gen
  • Power seats: Some 2020+ trims have power driver seats — check cover compatibility

What About the 4th Gen (2024+)?

The 2024 Tacoma is a complete redesign. Most seat cover manufacturers are still developing patterns for the 4th gen. We'll update this guide as options become available. For now, this guide covers the 2005–2023 models where Bartact has full coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do 2nd gen and 3rd gen Tacoma seat covers interchange?

No. The 2nd generation (2005–2015) and 3rd generation (2016–2023) have different seat dimensions and mounting points. Always buy covers specific to your generation.

What generation is my Toyota Tacoma?

2nd generation Tacomas are 2005–2015 models. 3rd generation Tacomas are 2016–2023 models. The 4th generation started with the 2024 model year.

Do I need different covers for Access Cab vs Double Cab?

Front seat covers are typically the same between cab styles. Rear seat covers differ — the Access Cab has fold-up jump seats while the Double Cab has a traditional bench. Make sure to select the right cab configuration when ordering.
